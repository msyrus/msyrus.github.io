01 / About

I've been writing code professionally for more than a decade — a trajectory that's taken me from Dhaka to Sofia to London, through Pathao, Musala Soft, Amazon, and now Sainsbury's Digital, Tech & Data, where I'm a Senior Software Engineer building backend systems in Go.

I cut my teeth on competitive programming — the habit of reaching for the right data structure before the right framework has stuck with me ever since. I published four peer-reviewed papers during my research-assistant years (IEEE + Elsevier, on wireless sensor networks and computer vision for road-safety in Bangladesh). These days I spend most of my time on distributed systems, event-driven architectures, and making them dull enough to sleep through.

On the side, I take on selective freelance work through the Toptal network (top 3% of global talent) and occasionally speak at the Golang Meetup Dhaka community. I also build small developer tools whenever something annoys me enough — a VS Code extension for Go, an IP watcher for dynamic DNS, a Raspberry Pi fan controller, and a handful of CLIs.

Away from the screen, I'm a practicing Muslim and a proud dad of two daughters — the best part of any day is the one I spend with them. It's also excellent training for debugging under pressure.
